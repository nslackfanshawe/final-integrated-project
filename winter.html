Wilderness Nature: Feed the Canada Jay & Chickadee

Canada's New Official Bird; the Canada Jay and of course the Chick-a-dee! Touching Wildlife.

Famous birds that will eat seeds from your hand or your hat are the Canada Jay (aka the Gray Jay or the Whisky Jack) and Boreal Chickadee. For the Aficionado Birder check the latest winter birding report for what is being seen and where.

  1. Algonquin Logging Museum Trail
  2. Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail
  3. Mew Lake Old Railway Trail

We Provide Free to Use for our guests:

  • Pro IceHiking Crampons and Walking Poles
  • Park Pass from our Office
  • Bird seeds
  • Please never scatter or leave seed behind on the trails.

Cross Country Skiing & Snow Shoeing

Tranquil with peacefulness.  Algonquin Park has International quality ski trails; track set and skating lane.

48 kilometres of groomed and 23 kilometres of non-groomed cross-country ski trails available in three low-usage trail networks, typically December to early April.  Check the latest ski trail conditions If you cannot go skiing you can always go IceHiking.

Provided Free to use:

  • Wax-less cross-country skis, boots and poles. 
  • Waxing station for those that enjoy waxing. 
  • Park Pass from our Office 
  • Pro snowshoes 
  • Park Pass from our Office 

Serene spots for Snowshoeing:

  1. Algonquin outdoor Logging Museum Trail 
  2. Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail 
  3. Mew Lake Old Railway Trail Go to Mew Lake Campground for starting point.

Algonquin Park

Algonquin Visitor Center

Pristine and natural snow covered Algonquin Park is home to world-class venues and we provide the free Park Pass. Algonquin Park's Visitor Centre is open daily, has exhibits on the Park's natural and human history, a relaxing restaurant, an excellent bookstore, theatre and a viewing deck of the Winter wild Algonquin landscape.
